Personal Background:

I am a seventh generation Coloradoan with a rich family history of ranching, hunting, and exploration. My great great great great grandpa was a man named Kit Carson, a famous 19th century explorer and mountain man who initially came to the west in search of beaver pelts. My grandpa's old adobe style home has been made into a museum in Taos, the town that he seemed to love the most and where he made his home during the later part of his life.

Hobbies:

I try to follow in my ancestor's footsteps by spending a lot of time in the mountains. During the summer months I enjoy rock climbing, mountaineering, fly fishing, shooting, kayaking, and backpacking. When I am in the wilderness I feel a profound connection to my heritage.

Professional Background:

I graduated from the University of Colorado in 2017 with a bachelor's degree in Environmental Biology. After college I worked for a civil engineering firm where I was a part of a geospatial team working on federal mapping contracts for the US Airforce as well as the US Geological Survey. After about 2 1/2 years I grew tired of working an office job and decided to move up to Steamboat Springs where I spent the winter managing a ski shop. During the summer I work for the US Forest Service as a forestry technician and wildland firefighter. I am currently trying to learn coding and development in order to enhance my ability to get back into the geospatial field and hopefully be able to work remotely so that I can move around without having to worry about finding a new job each time I move locations.
